# Becoming an SPVS Team Member

This document outlines the requirements and process for joining the SPVS core team. 
Contributors who meet these criteria and want a more active role in shaping the standard are encouraged to apply.

## What Team Membership Means

Team members have a direct role in the direction of SPVS. This includes:

- Voting on proposed controls and standard changes
- Reviewing and approving pull requests
- Participating in roadmap and release decisions
- Representing SPVS in community and OWASP contexts

Membership is distinct from contributing. You can contribute without being a team member, but team members are expected to do both.

## Requirements

### Contribution Quality and Volume

- A minimum of 10 accepted contributions to the SPVS repository
- Contributions must consist of substantive work: new control proposals, control overhauls, or major documentation changes. Simple edits, typo fixes, and formatting changes do not count foward this threshold
- Contributions must span a minimum of 2 to 3 months. A burst of PRs within a short window does not satisfy this requirement. We are looking for demonstrated sustained engagement

### Peer Review Participation

- You must have actively reviewed and commented on other contributors' pull requests. Submitting your own work is not sufficient. Team members are expected to evaluate incoming controls from others, and we need to see evidence of that before membership is granted

### Community Presence

- You must be an active member of [#owasp-spvs](https://owasp.slack.com/archives/C0AQW879656) on OWASP Slack
- Active means participating in discussions, not just being present. Lurking does not satisfy this requirement

### Vendor Neutrality

- You must disclose any employer, commercial, or vendor relationships that could represent a conflict of interest with the SPVS standard. This is a condition of membership, not a disqualifier. Undisclosed conflicts of interest are a disqualifier
- This aligns with standard OWASP project leadership expectations and protects the integrity of the standard

### Application

- Submit a short written statement to the team via the Slack channel or as a GitHub discussion. It should cover why you want to join the team and what you
  intend to contribute as a member
- Meeting the criteria above does not result in automatic membership. The existing team will review your application and vote. A simple majority is required for approval
- The team will respond within 30 days of your application

## After Joining

Team members are expected to remain active. Membership may be revoked or moved to emeritus status if a member becomes inactive for an extended period without notice.
What counts as inactive will be determined by the team at the time, but sustained absence from both GitHub and Slack with no communication is the primary indicator.

## Questions

Reach us in [#owasp-spvs](https://owasp.slack.com/archives/C0AQW879656) on OWASP Slack.

## Code of Conduct

We ask that all contributors to OWASP projects abide by our [Code of Conduct](https://owasp.org/www-policy/operational/code-of-conduct).
This code outlines our expectations for behavior within the project community and helps us maintain a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors.

Thank you for your interest in contributing to an OWASP project. We appreciate your efforts to help us improve and grow our projects.
